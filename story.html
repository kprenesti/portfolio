Krista Prenesti's Development Journey: The Story

It all started with...


It all started with a need and a book. The need came directly after the realization that teaching in a traditional classroom setting was no longer a promising proposition. The book came in the mail. It was an unassuming brochure from my alma mater's Continuing Education Department. I had gotten dozens of these in the mail before and all of them had wound up in the recycling bin, unopened. But perhaps it was the requirement for something new or the mantra of prayer for guidance that led me to open the book, but this time I clumsily ripped open the little tape that holds the book closed...and fumbled the book. It landed in my lap, open to the precise page advertising their web development course.

I have always been a researcher at heart. Show me a purchase prospect and by the end of the day I will know the product better than the producer of said product. So, naturally, before I forked out the required $5,000, I did my homework. Never have I been so thrilled with the results of homework, for I found that not only was the program in question inadequate, but that a majority of web developers are self-taught! And by using free or affordable resources! That same night, I discovered Codeacademy and the names of the first "languages" I'd learn: HTML and CSS. That same night, I went from ex-teacher to future web developer, and the future has never looked brighter!

Since then...

Since then I've acquired skills in HTML, CSS, JQuery, JavaScript, and PHP (of the WordPress variety). I have become a camper at Free Code Camp and have dabbled in The Odin Project. But my journey is not yet complete. I intend on learning Node.js and at least one other programming language. Ruby vs Python: the debate is hot and furious with no clear winner. Stay tuned to find out which direction I go. In the mean time, check out My Portfolio and My Medium Articles.
